Queuing for food

Dishoom or Hoppers - two very good London restaurants that get filled up very quickly

Queuing more than one hour to get a table in a restaurant, do you find it ok? I don’t think I do.
Do I think the food is worth more because I waited a long time? Definitely not.

The restaurant in question where we had to queue for more than one hour to get to the bar where we waited again to then get a table is Dishoom. Although Dishoom gives some refreshments while waiting outside, I still do not think it outweighs the pain of waiting.

We were a group of 7 and the friend organising called 3 weeks in advance to book a table and it was already fully booked in two restaurants whose locations were central enough for everyone. So be prepared to wait!

I have not a single remark to say about Dishoom’s food quality, freshness and tastiness but is it really worth the wait?

We are in an era where things should get faster and easier. Shouldn’t Dishoom adopt a better method?
Which brings me to my next point, the better method.

There are probably lots of good ideas out there for Dishoom not to make us wait in queues anymore and one of them is the Hoppers’ way.

Hoppers is another really really good restaurant, I am not here to compare the food in Hoppers and the food in Dishoom because I cannot compare apples and pears :) but I can compare their waiting method.

Both entities get extremely busy but at Hoppers, once you arrive, you present yourself at the hostess, she writes your name down and your phone number. She gives you an approximate waiting time and she then sends you a text when the table is going to be ready for you to present yourself at the restaurant again. This leaves you time to wander around the area or even to sit down at a cafe/bar near by depending on the waiting time.

Will I go back to Hoppers? Without a hesitation. Will I go back to Dishoom? Probably but I have to forget about the pain of waiting by then.
